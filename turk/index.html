Instructions:

Mark the Head and Tail positions for each image and answer image-related questions

  1. Find the head of the biological specimen in the column "Head Position"
    • Place a marker on the head with a (left-) click of your mouse.
    • If the head is out of frame, select the "Out of Frame" option, instead.
    • Refer to the example to distinguish head from tail.
  2. Find the tail of the biological specimen in the column "Tail Position"
    • Repeat step 1) for the tail
  3. Determine orientation of the specimen in column "Orientation"
    • Select "Facing Camera" if head is closer to the camera than tail, or "Back to Camera" otherwise.
    • Select "Parallel" if at roughly the same distance.
    • Select "Not Sure" if you are unsure of specimen orientation.
  4. Select the level of confidence of your "Head" and "Tail" annotations in column "Confidence Score".
  5. Click submit after HIT is complete.
