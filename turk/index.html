Mark the head and tail positions and answer image-related questions

Instructions:

Mark the Head and Tail positions for each image and answer image-related questions

Each row contains a different task. Follow the instructions below for each row.

  1. Find the head of the biological specimen in the column "Head Position"
    • Place a marker on the head with a (left-) click of your mouse.
    • If the head is out of frame, select the "Out of Frame" option, instead.
    • Refer to the example to distinguish head from tail.
  2. Find the tail of the biological specimen in the column "Tail Position"
    • Repeat step 1) for the tail
  3. Select the level of confidence of your "Head" and "Tail" annotations in column "Confidence Score".
  4. Determine orientation of the specimen in column "Orientation"
    • Select "Leaning towards camera" if head is closer to the camera than tail, or "Leaning away from camera" otherwise.
    • Select "Parallel" if at roughly the same distance.
    • Select "Not Sure" if you are unsure of specimen orientation.
  5. Select the focus of the images in column "Focus". Note that the head and tail images are the same.
  6. Click submit after HIT is complete.

NOTE: If the cursor does not appear over the image, refresh the page to fix it.
